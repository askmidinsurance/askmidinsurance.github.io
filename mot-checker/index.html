Frequently Asked Questions

What is MOT?

An MOT (Ministry of Transport) test is an annual safety and emissions inspection required for most vehicles in the UK. It confirms that your car meets the minimum legal standards for road safety and environmental requirements at the time of testing.

How do I check my MOT?

You can check your MOT by entering your vehicle registration number into an online MOT checker. This will instantly show your MOT expiry date and often includes your full MOT and mileage history.

When is my MOT due?

Your first MOT is due three years after your car is first registered, or four years if you are in Northern Ireland. After that, an MOT is required every year. You can find the exact due date by checking your MOT status online.

How do I know if a car has a valid MOT?

You can confirm whether a car has a valid MOT by checking its registration number online. This is especially useful when buying a used car to ensure it is legally allowed on the road.

Does a valid MOT mean my car is safe?

A valid MOT means your car met legal safety standards on the day it was tested. However, faults can develop at any time, so it‚Äôs your responsibility to make sure your car remains safe to drive.

Why is my MOT expiry date incorrect?

MOT records can take up to five days to update after your vehicle is tested. If your MOT was completed recently, the correct expiry date should appear once the system refreshes.

Can I drive my car if it fails an MOT?

You can drive your car after an MOT failure only if your existing MOT is still valid and no dangerous faults were found. If dangerous defects are listed, the car must not be driven until repaired.

What happens if my MOT has expired?

Driving without a valid MOT is illegal unless you are travelling to a pre-booked MOT test. You could face fines, penalty points, and invalid insurance if caught.

What do MOT tests check?

An MOT checks key safety and environmental components such as brakes, lights, tyres, steering, suspension, seatbelts, mirrors, windscreen wipers, exhaust system, fluids, and emissions. It does not test the engine, clutch, or gearbox.

What are MOT advisories?

Advisories highlight minor issues that are not serious enough to fail the MOT but may worsen over time if not repaired. Fixing them early can help prevent future failures.

How much does an MOT cost?

The maximum MOT fee for a car in the UK is ¬£54.85, although many garages charge less. Prices may vary depending on location and any additional services offered.

How much does an MOT retest cost?

If repairs are carried out at the same test centre, a retest is usually free within 10 days. If you take the car elsewhere and return within 10 days, a discounted retest may apply.

Is my vehicle exempt from an MOT?

Most vehicles need an MOT after three years, but some are exempt, including vehicles over 40 years old and certain specialist or electric vehicles. Always check your vehicle‚Äôs status to be sure.
